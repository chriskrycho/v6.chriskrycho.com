I’m a Christian — Reformed, Anglican, and catholic, in that order. I am also a husband, and the father of two wonderful young women.

By trade, I am a software engineer:

  • I spent a few years as the tech lead for LinkedIn’s desktop web experience.
  • I developed cutting-edge research on TypeScript types and semantic versioning.
  • I founded the Ember.js TypeScript team, and led it for 6 years.

I spend a lot of my time thinking and publishing — not only on technology, but also art, culture, and especially theology. I have been published in Plough Quarterly, Mere Orthodoxy, and The Gospel Coalition. I have spoken at a variety of conferences, including BibleTech, Rust Belt Rust, EmberConf, All Things Open, and in 2024, LambdaConf.

I am also an active composer, with recent works ranging from small chamber pieces written for friends to a fanfare recorded with the Budapest Scoring Orchestra.

Finally, I am also an avid runner and cyclist, and keep up a semi-serious photography hobby.
