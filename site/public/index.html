I’m a Christian—Reformed, Anglican, and catholic—, a husband, and the father of two wonderful young women.

By trade, I am a software engineer:

  • I spent a few years as the tech lead for LinkedIn’s desktop web experience.
  • I developed cutting-edge research on TypeScript types and semantic versioning
  • I founded the Ember.js TypeScript team, and led it for 6 years.

I spend a lot of my time thinking and publishing on not only technology but also art, culture, and—especially!—theology. I have been published in Plough Quarterly, Mere Orthodoxy, and The Gospel Coalition. I have spoken at a variety of conferences, including BibleTech, Rust Belt Rust, EmberConf, All Things Open, and in 2024, LambdaConf.

I am also a working composer, with pieces ranging from small chamber pieces written for friends, to the themes for my podcasts, to a fanfare recorded with the Budapest Scoring Orchestra.

Finally, I am also an avid runner and cyclist, and keep up a semi-serious photography hobby.
